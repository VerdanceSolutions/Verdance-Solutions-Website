The Friction You Encounter

Most specialty food buyers face the same barriers when sourcing directly from Mexico at scale.

Regulatory Exposure

Incomplete FSVP files, vague supplier documentation, and inconsistent labeling quietly accumulate into audit and detention risk.

Unstable Supply

Informal producer relationships translate into missed sailings, unstable lead times, and inventory buffers that eat margin.

Quality Degradation

When product specifications are not actively managed at origin, flavor, moisture, and grading slowly degrade across seasons.

Working Capital Drain

Many importers float 60–90 days of inventory cost before seeing revenue. That capital requirement limits how much volume you can move.

Stacked Markups

Traditional broker networks add markups on top of distributor margins, compressing your ability to compete on quality.

Concentration Risk

Relying on one producer or intermediary concentrates supply-chain risk in a single relationship.
